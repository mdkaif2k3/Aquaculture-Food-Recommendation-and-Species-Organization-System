About Aquaculture

Aquaculture refers to the controlled cultivation of aquatic organisms such as fish, crustaceans, and aquatic plants in freshwater, brackish, or marine environments. As global demand for fish continues to rise, aquaculture has become one of the fastest-growing food production sectors, playing a critical role in food security and sustainable protein supply.

This project aims to address these challenges by combining structured data management, graph-based visualization, and a data-driven recommendation system. The system provides a unified platform where users can explore fish species, understand their ecological relationships, and receive informed food recommendations based on farming conditions.

By integrating data from multiple sources and applying analytical techniques such as Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), the platform supports better decision-making for aquaculture practitioners. Instead of relying solely on experience or trial-and-error, farmers can use data-backed insights to choose suitable feed, improve fish health, and optimize overall productivity.

Platform Features

Fish Species Knowledge Base

The system maintains a structured and searchable database of fish species commonly used in aquaculture. Each species entry includes essential biological and ecological information such as taxonomy, habitat preference, water type suitability, and conservation status. This centralized knowledge base helps users avoid ambiguity caused by multiple common names and provides consistent, reliable reference data for decision-making.

Relationship Visualization

Complex relationships between fish species, habitats, and food sources are represented using graphical visualizations. These relationship graphs allow users to explore how species are connected within the aquaculture ecosystem, making it easier to identify compatible species, shared habitats, and feeding patterns. This graphical representation enhances understanding beyond what traditional tables or lists can offer.

Intelligent Food Recommendation System

The platform provides food recommendations tailored to specific fish species and farming conditions. By combining fish species data with harvester-provided information such as farm type, water type, and scale of operation, the system predicts suitable feed options that support healthy growth and efficient farming. This reduces dependency on trial-and-error approaches and minimizes manual decision-making errors.

Harvester Profile & Farm Context

Each registered user maintains a profile containing farm-related details including farm type, farm size, and water conditions. This contextual information allows the system to personalize recommendations and ensures that insights are relevant to the user’s actual aquaculture environment. The profile also serves as a centralized view of user-specific data within the platform.
